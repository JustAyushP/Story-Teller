Broken Brilliance

Fog clung to the spires of New College as Victor Hollow paused, tugging at his cuff, then his collar, then his cuff again, as if either would sit properly for once. The wool of his coat was wet, his shoes had taken water, and his hair—usually combed respectably—hung in an uncooperative curl over his forehead.

Hardly the entrance of a genius, he thought, though as he caught sight of a fellow student hurrying past, Victor straightened at once. A shrugged smirk hit his face, quick and polished. His eyes sharpened. To anyone looking, he was perfectly in command, a man destined for brilliance.

But the leather folio tucked under his arm told another story: papers covered in revisions, margins scarred with hasty erasures. Pages had been rewritten, ideas reshuffled in the small hours of sleepless nights. He clutched it tighter as if the whole thing might fall apart in his hands.

Glooming ahead was the great hall of the university, lanterns burning dimly against the cobblestone walls. Behind those firm wooden doors lay the Fellows, the Board, and the promise, or ruin, of his career.

Victor drew a breath, squared his shoulders, and stepped forward. As he edged closer, the sound of his footsteps grew louder and louder.

Today they will know me. One way or another.
